Reading Fluency

When your child reads out loud, how would you describe their reading?
Reading fluency refers to how smoothly and accurately your child reads out loud.
Very choppy and slow - Reads word-by-word with frequent pauses and struggles with most words
Somewhat choppy - Reads in short phrases but pauses often and struggles with longer words
Mostly smooth - Generally reads in phrases with appropriate expression but occasionally struggles with unfamiliar words
Very smooth and expressive - Reads with appropriate phrasing and expression, rarely struggles with words

Word Recognition

How easily can your child recognize common words without having to sound them out?
Sight words are common words that readers should recognize instantly (like "the," "and," "what").
Struggles with many common words - Needs to sound out even frequently used words
Recognizes some common words - Can instantly recognize basic words but sounds out many others
Recognizes most common words - Only needs to sound out unfamiliar or complex words
Excellent word recognition - Instantly recognizes most age-appropriate words

Reading Comprehension

After reading, how well can your child understand and recall what they've read?
Reading comprehension is the ability to understand, remember, and explain what has been read.
Significant difficulty - Can't recall main details or answer basic questions about what was read
Some understanding - Can recall some details but struggles to explain main ideas or make connections
Good understanding - Can recall main ideas and details but may miss deeper meanings or connections
Excellent comprehension - Fully understands what was read, can discuss, make connections, and draw conclusions

Phonics Skills

How well can your child sound out unfamiliar words?
Phonics is the ability to connect letters with sounds and blend those sounds to read unfamiliar words.
Significant difficulty - Cannot or rarely attempts to sound out unfamiliar words
Inconsistent - Can sound out some simple words but struggles with more complex words or patterns
Generally effective - Can sound out most age-appropriate words but may struggle with complex patterns
Very effective - Confidently sounds out unfamiliar words using phonics rules

Reading Enjoyment

How does your child feel about reading?
A child's attitude toward reading often indicates their comfort level with reading skills.
Strongly dislikes reading - Avoids reading, gets frustrated or upset when asked to read
Reluctant reader - Will read when required but doesn't choose to read for pleasure
Generally enjoys reading - Doesn't mind reading and sometimes chooses to read for pleasure
Loves reading - Regularly chooses to read for pleasure and gets excited about books

At grade level - Meeting the expected reading benchmarks for their grade
Above grade level - Exceeding the expected reading benchmarks for their grade
Not sure - I haven't received specific feedback about my child's reading level

Family History

Is there a family history of reading difficulties, dyslexia, or other learning challenges?
Reading challenges often have a genetic component.
Yes - One or more close family members (parents, siblings) have diagnosed reading difficulties or dyslexia
Possibly - Family members have struggled with reading but were never formally diagnosed
No - No known family history of reading difficulties or dyslexia
Not sure - I don't have enough information about family history
